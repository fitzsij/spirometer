The Challenge:

Design a peak flow meter to be used in the developing world

Our Approach

We first looked up where the most pollution is in the world -- China

Xianjing is one of China's poorest and most remote regions, which combined with heavy industrial and ecological air degradation means it has some of the worst health outcomes for Chinese people

Asthma and other ailments which a PFM can help detect are common there

A change in direction...

One of our team members has a cousin working as a doctor in Lagos, Nigeria

We investigated and found this was a clear case where our design could make and intervention:

  • Poverty & overcrowding
  • Less extensive health system
  • Astham and other diseases common
  • Insider connection!

Design Brief

To design a device to measure and monitor peak flow rates. The device is to be used in high populated areas where there is a low number of consultants relevant to cases presented. The peak flow monitoring must be functional in areas where there is no access to large scale data recording facilities. The operation must be developed to take pressure off clinicians in these areas.

The device is intended to be used by educators and children in the age bracket of 5-14 years old. This device will be used a part of a scheme to reduce asthma in these regions

Our Solution

Our product is easy to use, simple, cheap, effective and safe.

Usage Instructions

Step 1: Relax and Breath Easy :)

Pick up the Spirometer upright and put the mouthpiece to your lips

Blow as hard as you can -- the ball will float higher depending on how hard your lungs work

What reading did you get? Write it down along with what time it is on paper or on our app

Take a look at your chart and follow the instructions you get
